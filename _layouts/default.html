Juyoun Yoo

I am a PhD student in Larry Zipursky lab at UCLA. I entered Neuroscience IDP program in 2015, and joined the Zipursky group in 2016. I earned my Master’s degree in Biological Science from Seoul National University in 2015. I worked with Bong-kiun Kaang group to study learning, memory and social behavior of Autism Spectrum Disorder mice models. I did my Batchelors at Ewha Womans University from Seoul, Korea.

I'm generally interested in how the brain is built.
During my phD I investigated how neuronal gene expressions are changing in the course of neurogenesis and synaptogenesis, how cell-surface molecules including neurotransmitter receptors are related to synaptic specificity. I have experience with single-cell RNA sequencing, immunohistochemistry, confocal imaging, CRISPR & Drosophila genetics.

Work during my Ph.D in Zipursky lab

Transcriptional Programs of Circuit Assembly in the Drosophila Visual System

Yerbol Z. Kurmangaliyev,

Juyoun Yoo

, Javier Valdes-Aleman, Piero Sanfilippo, S. Lawrence Zipursky
Neuron, 2020

We generated developmental transcriptome atlas of the whole fly opticlobe throughout the pupal stage. We showed that there are pan-neuronal programs that is common for the majority of neurons, and also there are cell-type specific programs. We strategized to use Drosophila Genome Reference Panel(DGRP) to multiplex the library to minimize batch effect in a simple and cost-effective manner.

Modular transcriptional programs separately define axon and dendrite connectivity
